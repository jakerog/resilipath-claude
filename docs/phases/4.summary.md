## Objective
Deliver a multi-channel mass notification and crisis communication system that enables organizations to rapidly notify all stakeholder tiers — employees, vendors, contractors, government bodies, regulatory agencies, customers, and media — during a declared crisis or disaster. This module replaces ad-hoc email blasts, phone trees, and manual calling scripts with a structured, tracked, auditable communication engine.

## Why This Module Is Critical
During the real disasters that BCPs and DR exercises prepare for, communication failure is often the second failure after the primary incident. Organizations routinely discover during actual disasters that:
- They cannot reach employees at scale in minutes
- They don't know who has acknowledged notifications and who hasn't
- Regulatory notifications are missed, triggering compliance penalties
- Inconsistent messages go to different stakeholder groups, causing confusion
- There's no record of what was communicated and when (critical for insurance and legal)

This module solves all of these problems.

## Core Architecture

### Incident Lifecycle
```
Declare Incident → Activate Communication Plan → Send Notifications (multi-channel)
    → Track Acknowledgements → Escalate Non-Responses → Log Everything
    → Send Updates → Declare Resolution → Generate Communication Report
```

### Notification Channels
- **Email** (high volume, detailed content, audit trail)
- **SMS** (short-form, high urgency, broad reach including non-email users)
- **Push Notification** (app users — highest immediacy)
- **In-App Alert** (platform users monitoring the incident)
- **Voice Call** (robocall — for critical notifications to non-smartphone users; via Twilio)
- **Webhook** (for Slack/Teams/PagerDuty integration — machine-readable events)

### Stakeholder Contact Groups
The module manages contact groups representing every stakeholder tier:
- **Employees** (by facility, department, role, shift)
- **Managers** (by level, function, facility)
- **Vendors & Contractors** (by criticality tier, service type)
- **Executive Leadership** (board, C-suite)
- **Government Bodies** (local emergency management, fire, police)
- **Regulatory Agencies** (OSHA, EPA, FDA, SEC, FTC, state regulators)
- **Insurance** (brokers, adjusters, carriers)
- **Customers** (key accounts, all customers, by region)
- **Media** (PR/communications team, approved media contacts)
- **Community** (neighbors, community liaisons)

### Communication Plans
A Communication Plan is a pre-authored playbook for a specific scenario type (fire, cyber breach, pandemic, etc.) that defines:
- Which contact groups to notify
- In what sequence/cascade
- Via which channels
- Using which message templates
- At what time intervals
- Who must acknowledge before the next tier is notified

### Regulatory Notification Intelligence
The system maintains a registry of regulatory reporting obligations (by industry, jurisdiction, incident type) and:
- Alerts the user when a declared incident triggers a mandatory reporting requirement
- Presents the applicable regulation (e.g., OSHA 300, SEC 8-K, GDPR 72-hour rule)
- Provides a pre-populated notification template
- Tracks submission status and deadline

## Integration Dependencies
- Module 2 (BCP) contact directory feeds this module's contact groups
- Module 1 (DR Exercise) incident declaration can trigger a communication plan
- Email engine from Phase 2 is extended (not rebuilt) for mass notification

## Success Criteria
- 1,000 notifications sent in under 60 seconds
- Acknowledgement tracking works across email (link click), SMS (reply), and push
- Regulatory notification alerts trigger correctly for 5+ scenario/jurisdiction combinations
- Communication audit report is legally defensible and exportable within 60 seconds of request
- Cascade/tree notification executes in correct sequence (manager tier → team tier → all-staff)
